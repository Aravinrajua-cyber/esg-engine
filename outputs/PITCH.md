# PITCH — ESG Momentum Engine (7 minutes)

> **Fill tokens.** Anything in `‹FROZEN: …›` is read from `data/processed/phase4_results.pkl` /
> `outputs/site_data/companies.json` after the Phase 4 freeze. Do not invent these — paste the real

**One-line thesis (say it twice — open and close):**
*"Static ESG scores are slow; the change in a company's ESG signal is what predicts returns — and in
ASEAN, where coverage is thin, the market underreacts to that change."*

---

## 0:00 — Hook (45s)
**On screen:** title slide, then the provider-divergence stat.
**Say:** "Ask six major ESG providers how sustainable a company is and they agree about as often as
a coin flip and a half — the average pairwise correlation of their ratings is **0.54** (Berg, Kölbel
& Rigobon, *Aggregate Confusion*, 2022). If the *level* is that noisy, betting on the level is
betting on noise. So we stopped trying to measure how green a company *is*, and started measuring how
fast it's *getting greener*."
**Landing line:** "Level is contested. Momentum is observable."

## 0:45 — Thesis (30s)
**On screen:** the 2×2 — ESG level (x) vs ESG momentum (y), four quadrants labeled.
**Say:** "Our product finds **Hidden Winners**: companies with an unremarkable ESG *level* but
strong, improving ESG *momentum* — the names the static-score crowd hasn't repriced yet. We rank all
**477 ASEAN companies** across five markets, and we prove the signal on the **198 most liquid,
tradeable names**."

## 1:15 — Live demo (2:00) — *this is the heart; rehearse the exact path in DEMO.md*
**On screen:** the live app.
1. **Leaderboard** — "477 names, ranked by ESG-momentum score, with a grade, a confidence band, and a
   data-coverage figure on every row. Nothing here is a black box."
2. **Search the pre-picked Hidden Winner** `‹FROZEN: HIDDEN_WINNER_NAME›` — click into detail.
3. **Explanation sentence** — "This sentence is generated by a deterministic rule engine, not an LLM:
   it names the two strongest positive drivers and the strongest negative one. A judge — or a client
   — can trace the score to its inputs in one read."
4. **Risk flags + confidence band** — "Low coverage, rising controversy, low liquidity, high
   volatility, stale data — each flagged with a tooltip. The confidence band *widens* when the
   evidence is thin. We show our uncertainty instead of hiding it."
5. **Weight sandbox** (10s) — drag a slider — "If you disagree with our weights, re-rank live. Reset
   restores the validated model. The objection 'it's just your weights' becomes a feature."
**Landing line:** "Every number on this screen traces to a source or a formula."

## 4:15 — The placebo: "we tried to kill our own result" (1:00)
**On screen:** `placebo_histogram.png` — 1,000 shuffled-composite spreads near zero, realized spread
marked far in the right tail.
**Say:** "Before we believed our own backtest, we tried to break it. We shuffled the scores across
companies 1,000 times — destroying any real signal — and re-ran the whole backtest each time. That's
this distribution, centered on zero. Our real result is **here** — p = **‹FROZEN: placebo_p›**. And
because we tested many configurations, we report the **Deflated Sharpe Ratio** (Bailey & López de
Prado, 2014), which discounts for the search: **‹FROZEN: deflated_sharpe›**. We applied
Benjamini–Hochberg false-discovery control at q=0.10 across ~72 hypotheses, because most 'factors'
die under honest multiple-testing (Harvey, Liu & Zhu, 2016)."
**Landing line:** "Anyone can show you a backtest. We're showing you the autopsy we ran on ours."

## 5:15 — The honest risk slide (45s)
**On screen:** `risks` section — severity-graded cards.
**Say:** "Three things we want you to know. One: our free ESG data source **died mid-build** — Yahoo
pulled the Sustainalytics feed. We didn't paper over it; the model runs on alternative data, which is
**exactly the resilience argument we're making**. Two: shorting is infeasible in much of ASEAN, so
the implementable result is **long-only top-quintile vs benchmark**, and we report that separately.
Three: survivorship is present by construction; it's registered and directionally bounded. The limits
are documented, not hidden."
**Landing line:** "This is real data with real limits. The limits are the story."

## 6:00 — Upgrade path → CGS (45s)
**On screen:** architecture slide — free pipeline today, CSV/API import for licensed data.
**Say:** "Today this runs on free data. The single biggest unlock is **point-in-time ESG history** —
which we don't have for free, but **CGS does**. Drop a licensed feed into our CSV/API import layer —
same frozen schemas, no re-engineering — and the proxied ESG-momentum variable becomes a *true*

**Landing line:** "We didn't build a toy. We built the socket your data plugs into."
